Biography

Ratan Tata, born on December 28, 1937, in Bombay (now Mumbai), India, is an iconic Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons. He is a member of the prominent Tata family, a family synonymous with the growth and development of modern industry in India.

Ratan Tata comes from a family with a long history of philanthropy and business leadership. He is the son of Naval Tata and Soonoo Commisariat and the great-grandson of Tata Group founder, Jamsetji Tata. He received his early education at the Campion School in Mumbai and later pursued architecture and structural engineering studies at Cornell University and Harvard Business School.

Ratan Tata began his career with the Tata Group in 1962. He initially worked on the shop floor of Tata Steel and gradually moved through various positions within the company. He was deeply influenced by the values and ethos of the Tata Group and its commitment to societal welfare.

In 1991, Ratan Tata became the Chairman of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group. Under his leadership, the Tata Group expanded globally, acquiring major international companies and reinforcing its presence in various industries such as automobiles, steel, information technology, telecommunications, and more.

One of Ratan Tata's most notable achievements was overseeing the acquisition of major global brands like Jaguar Land Rover, Tetley, and Corus during his tenure. He is credited with steering the Tata Group into the league of global conglomerates.

Ratan Tata's leadership style has always been characterized by a focus on innovation, ethics, and sustainable development. He emphasized the importance of corporate social responsibility, and the Tata Group under his guidance engaged in several philanthropic activities and social initiatives.
